Bathing 101

What to know before you step inside

The three things that make Western visitors hesitate at the door — answered plainly, so you can walk in like you belong.

01

Yes, you bathe nude

Bathing nude is the norm in communal baths, which are single-sex almost everywhere. A small towel is fine for modesty — just keep it out of the water. For most first-timers, the awkwardness fades quickly.

02

The tattoo question

Many baths still refuse tattoos — a legacy rule, not personal. Some welcome them openly; others accept approved cover patches or offer reservable private baths. We map the ones we've verified.

03

Wash before you soak

The tub is for soaking, never washing. Sit, scrub and rinse fully at the shower stations first — the one rule that matters most in communal bathing.
